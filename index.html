About Me

I’m a Brooklyn-based social scientist with a background in cognitive science and psychology. I hold a Master’s degree from NYU’s Applied Statistics for Social Science Research (A3SR) program, where I learned to blend advanced statistical modeling with human stories to better understand behavior and social systems.

I have over 8 years of experience using quantitative and qualitative research methods, statistical modeling, and data visualization to explore how people think, act, and interact within social contexts. Working primarily in R, I explore topics including language and reasoning, social categorization, and ethical, human-centered approaches to technology.

I am currently the Quantitative Reasoning Specialist at the CUNY School of Labor and Urban Studies and previously taught Statistics for Social Change there. I also oversee the management of recruitment database systems for BabyChildTeen at NYU.

In my free time, I enjoy hosting elaborate dinner parties, taking technique-less dance classes, and after work trips to the dog park with my dog, Margie. Another passion of mine is design work and illustration, which naturally makes its way into most projects I work on. You can find a few examples of my more creative work here.
