About Me

I’m a Brooklyn-based social scientist with a background in cognitive science and psychology. I’m currently pursuing a master’s degree in NYU’s Applied Statistics for Social Science Research (A3SR) program, where I use advanced statistical and computational tools to answer socially impactful questions.

I have over 8 years of experience using quantitative and qualitative research methods, statistical modeling, and data visualization to explore how people think, act, and interact within social contexts. Working primarily in R, I explore topics including language and reasoning, social categorization, and ethical, human-centered approaches to technology.

In my free time, I enjoy hosting dinner parties, taking technique-less dance classes, and after work trips to the dog park with my dog, Margie.
